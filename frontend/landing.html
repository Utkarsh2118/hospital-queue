How it works

Three steps, start to finish

  1. 1

    Check in at the kiosk

    Pick a department, add basic details, and flag it as an emergency if needed. A token prints instantly.

  2. 2

    Watch it move, live

    Scan the QR code or open the tracking page to see queue position and estimated wait update in real time.

  3. 3

    Get called by the doctor

    When it's time, the token is called on the waiting-room display and the doctor's dashboard — no names shouted across the room.

Security & access

Staff access is locked down. Patients never need an account.

Patients check in without creating an account or storing a password, which keeps the system's attack surface small. Every doctor and admin account is protected the same way.

Hashed credentials

Passwords are hashed, never stored in plain text — for every doctor and admin account.

Verified sessions

Sessions are checked on every request, not just at login, so a stale token can't act on the queue.

Scoped by department

Doctors can only see and act on their own department's queue — never another team's patients.

Revocable access

Admins can deactivate a staff account instantly if access needs to be revoked.

Questions

Before you check in

Do patients need to create an account?

No. Patients check in with basic details at the kiosk and get a token — no account, no password, nothing to remember.

How does an emergency case skip the line?

Flagging a check-in as an emergency routes it to the front of that department's queue automatically, ahead of regular tokens.

Can one department's backlog slow down another?

No. Each department keeps its own independent queue and token counter, so Cardiology running long never delays Pediatrics.

How do patients know when it's their turn?

The waiting-room display and the patient's own tracking page (reached via the printed QR code) update in real time as tokens are called.

What can a doctor see versus an admin?

Doctors see and manage only their own department's queue. Admins see every department, every doctor, and every token, and can manage staff access.
